About the forum

Building a bridge from cryptographic research to financial impact

Cryptography is becoming foundational to modern financial systems, underpinning digital payments, digital identity, tokenised assets, central bank digital currencies, privacy-preserving services, regulatory technology and next-generation digital infrastructure.

AsiaCryptoFin 2026 will create a dedicated platform for researchers and practitioners to discuss deployment, industrial adoption, regulation and future research directions. The forum complements ASIACRYPT's technical programme by focusing on the path from strong cryptographic ideas to secure, scalable and trusted financial systems.

Why ASIACRYPT? ASIACRYPT is the premier cryptography conference in the Asia-Pacific region and a leading international venue for fundamental advances in cryptographic research. As an affiliated event, AsiaCryptoFin 2026 extends this mission by connecting the cryptography community with financial institutions, FinTech companies, regulators and policymakers, creating a bridge between cutting-edge research, practical deployment and real-world impact in finance.

Call for contributions

Share a talk that can shape the future of secure finance

We invite concise and engaging talk proposals from academia, financial institutions, FinTech companies, regulators, central banks, technology providers and digital-asset infrastructure organisations.

01

Research insight

Present a significant cryptographic development and explain its relevance, readiness and potential impact for financial systems.

02

Deployment case study

Share lessons from implementing cryptography in banking, payments, digital identity, digital assets, compliance or financial infrastructure.

03

Industry challenge

Frame an important technical, operational or regulatory challenge where collaboration with the cryptography community is needed.

04

Future perspective

Offer a forward-looking view on quantum transition, privacy, AI-enabled finance, tokenisation or the next generation of trusted infrastructure.

Submission format

Talk proposals only

No full paper is required and no formal proceedings are planned. Proposed talks should be suitable for a focused presentation of approximately 15-20 minutes; the final duration will be confirmed with accepted speakers.

Please include

  • Talk title and 200-300 word abstract
  • Speaker name, affiliation and short biography
  • Academic, industry, government or practitioner perspective
  • Key takeaway for the cryptography and finance communities
  • Optional links to related work, deployment or public material
